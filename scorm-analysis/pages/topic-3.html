From Hazard to Risk: The Assessment Process

Once a hazard is identified, you must assess the level of risk it presents. Risk is a combination of Likelihood (the chance of an incident happening) and Severity (the potential consequences). This is often visualized using a risk matrix.

The Risk Assessment Steps:

  1. Identify Hazards: What could go wrong? (e.g., leaking valve).
  2. Assess Risks: How likely is it to leak? What would be the consequences (a small fire, a large explosion)?
  3. Implement Controls: What can we do to reduce the risk? This follows the Hierarchy of Controls.
  4. Review and Monitor: Are the controls working? Has anything changed?

Hierarchy of Controls:

This is a system for selecting the most effective control measures. From most to least effective:

  • Elimination: Physically remove the hazard. (Most effective)
  • Substitution: Replace the hazard with something less hazardous.
  • Engineering Controls: Isolate people from the hazard (e.g., ventilation systems, gas detection alarms).
  • Administrative Controls: Change the way people work (e.g., safe work permits, procedures, warning signs).
  • PPE: Protect the worker with Personal Protective Equipment. (Least effective, last line of defense)
