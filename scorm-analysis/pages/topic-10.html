It's How We Do Things: Creating a Safety Culture

A 'safety culture' refers to the shared attitudes, beliefs, values, and behaviors related to safety within an organization. It's 'the way we do things around here.' It is the single most important factor in long-term safety performance.

Reactive vs. Proactive Culture:

Safety is seen as a priority that can be traded for production.Safety is a core value that is never compromised.Incidents are seen as failures of individuals. Blame is common.Incidents are seen as failures of the system. The focus is on learning.Management talks about safety, but actions don't match.Leadership is actively and visibly committed to safety.Reporting is discouraged or punished.Reporting is encouraged and rewarded.'We've always done it this way.''How can we do this more safely?'

Your Role in the Safety Culture:

Every individual contributes to the culture. You can strengthen it by:

  • Taking personal responsibility for your own safety and the safety of others.
  • Never taking shortcuts and always following procedures.
  • Speaking up when you see an unsafe act or condition.
  • Actively participating in safety meetings and training.
  • Encouraging your peers to work safely.
