What to Do When You Smell Gas

If you suspect a gas leak, you must act immediately and correctly. The wrong action can be catastrophic. Follow the Stop, Leave, Report protocol.

1. STOP What You're Doing

  • Do NOT operate any electrical switches, including lights.
  • Do NOT use a telephone or cell phone.
  • Do NOT start any vehicles or motors.
  • Do NOT smoke or create any other ignition source.
  • Extinguish any open flames if it is safe to do so.

2. LEAVE the Area Immediately

  • Warn others and evacuate the building or work area.
  • Leave doors open as you exit to help with ventilation.
  • Move to a safe, upwind location far from the suspected leak. Establish a designated assembly point.

3. REPORT the Leak

  • From a safe distance, call 911 and your company's emergency number or the local gas utility.
  • Provide your name, the location of the suspected leak, and any other observations (smell, sound, etc.).
  • Do not return to the area until emergency responders or qualified personnel have declared it safe.
