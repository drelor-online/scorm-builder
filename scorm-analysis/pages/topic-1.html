Understanding Natural Gas

Natural gas is primarily composed of methane (CH4). In its natural state, it is colorless, odorless, and tasteless. To aid in detection, a pungent odorant called mercaptan is added, which gives it a smell similar to rotten eggs. Understanding its properties is the first step to safety.

Key Properties:

  • Flammability: Natural gas is flammable only within a specific range of gas-to-air concentration, typically between 5% and 15%. Below 5%, it's too lean to burn; above 15%, it's too rich.
  • Density: It is lighter than air. If a leak occurs in an open area, it will typically rise and dissipate. In an enclosed space, it can accumulate, especially near the ceiling.
  • Asphyxiation Risk: While not toxic, natural gas can displace oxygen in a confined space, leading to asphyxiation.

The fundamental safety principle is to prevent the uncontrolled release of gas and to eliminate potential ignition sources in areas where gas could be present.
