Gearing Up: Your Last Line of Defense

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) is equipment worn to minimize exposure to hazards. While it's the last line of defense in the Hierarchy of Controls, its correct selection and use are non-negotiable.

Standard PPE for Natural Gas Work:

Flame-Resistant Clothing (FRC)Resists ignition and self-extinguishes once the heat source is removed. It is NOT fireproof but provides critical seconds to escape a flash fire.Hard HatProtects against falling objects and head impacts.Safety Glasses/GogglesProtects eyes from flying debris, dust, and chemical splashes.Safety-Toed BootsProtects feet from falling objects, punctures, and provides stable footing.GlovesProtects hands from cuts, abrasions, and chemicals. The type of glove depends on the specific task.

Specialized PPE:

In some situations, more specialized PPE may be required, such as:

  • Hearing Protection: In noisy environments like compressor stations.
  • Respiratory Protection: When entering known low-oxygen or contaminated environments.
  • Personal Gas Monitor: A device that alarms when it detects flammable gases or low oxygen levels.

Always inspect your PPE before each use. Damaged PPE must be replaced immediately.
