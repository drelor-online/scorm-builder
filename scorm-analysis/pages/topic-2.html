Sensing Danger: How to Identify Hazards

Hazard identification is an active, not a passive, process. You must use your senses and available tools to constantly survey your work environment. The primary hazard is an uncontrolled release of gas, which creates a risk of fire, explosion, or asphyxiation.

Signs of a Gas Leak:

  1. Smell: The most common sign is the distinct odor of mercaptan (rotten eggs).
  2. Sound: Listen for a hissing or roaring sound near a gas line or appliance.
  3. Sight: Look for blowing dirt, bubbling water, or dead/discolored vegetation in a specific spot over a buried gas line. You might also see a frosted area on a pipe, which can indicate a high-pressure leak.

Common Ignition Sources:

An ignition source is anything that can provide enough energy to ignite the gas-air mixture. Be vigilant for:

  • Open flames, sparks from tools, welding arcs
  • Cigarettes or other smoking materials
  • Static electricity discharge
  • Non-intrinsically safe electrical equipment (lights, phones, radios)
  • Starting vehicle engines

Knowledge Check

A patch of dead grass in a line over a buried pipe could be an indicator of a natural gas leak.
