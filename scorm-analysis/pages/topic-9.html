Knowledge in Action: Training and Drills

Safety knowledge is only effective when it can be applied correctly, especially under pressure. This is why ongoing training and practical drills are essential components of any safety program.

Types of Safety Training:

  • Initial Training: Comprehensive training for new hires before they begin work.
  • Refresher Training: Periodic training to reinforce knowledge and introduce new procedures or regulations. Most safety certifications require regular refreshers.
  • Task-Specific Training: Specialized training for non-routine or high-risk jobs, such as confined space entry or hot work.
  • Emergency Drill Training: Hands-on practice of emergency response procedures.

The Importance of Drills:

Reading about an emergency procedure is not the same as practicing it. Drills are designed to:

  • Build 'muscle memory' so that correct actions become automatic in a real emergency.
  • Identify weaknesses in the emergency plan (e.g., a blocked evacuation route, unclear communication).
  • Ensure emergency equipment is in good working order and that personnel know how to use it.
  • Clarify roles and responsibilities for the emergency response team.

Regular, realistic drills are the best way to prepare for a worst-case scenario.

Knowledge Check

Once you've completed initial safety training, refresher courses are generally not necessary.
