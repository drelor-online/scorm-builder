Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

Personal Protective Equipment is essential for electrical safety. Workers must wear appropriate PPE including insulated gloves, safety glasses, hard hats, and arc-rated clothing when working with electrical systems. Each piece of equipment serves a specific purpose in protecting against electrical hazards.

Hazard Identification and Risk Assessment

Identifying electrical hazards is crucial for workplace safety. Common hazards include exposed conductors, damaged equipment, overloaded circuits, and improper grounding. Workers must be trained to recognize these hazards and understand the associated risks before beginning any electrical work.

Lockout/Tagout Procedures

Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures are mandatory when servicing electrical equipment. These procedures ensure that energy sources are properly isolated and cannot be accidentally re-energized while work is being performed. Only authorized personnel should perform LOTO procedures.

Emergency Response Procedures

In the event of an electrical emergency, quick and proper response can save lives. Know the location of emergency shutdown procedures, first aid equipment, and emergency contact numbers. Never attempt to rescue someone who is in contact with an energized electrical source without first de-energizing the circuit.

Finally, let's discuss emergency response procedures. Quick thinking and proper response can literally save lives in electrical emergencies.

Know where your emergency shutoffs are located, where first aid equipment is stored, and who to contact in an emergency. Never touch someone who is in contact with live electricity - de-energize the circuit first.
